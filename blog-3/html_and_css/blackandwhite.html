Tulip Pandey

Black And White

A fresh slate lay, in front of him ,
And the poet,  charged with a special chore;
To create a lovely winter night,
That would, for want, leave no more.

After creating a masterpiece,
In a pensive dream he began to drown.
In oblivion he spilled the ink;
Now, darkness engulfed half the  town.

But art has never known no death,
His words, they refused to sink.
Light and bright , they remained afloat,
Like Crystals adrift  ,in a pool of ink.

The ink- the sky, the crystals-the stars;
At night their beauty continues to thrive.
The night-bleak black, the canvas-snow white,
No black and white, so full of life .
