Follower

I walked through black;
Dark, vacant and lifeless.
In search of an opening,
I walked deeper into the mess .

A branch tore my skin.
I tripped over a stone.
I followed a thousand steps,
But only alone.

“The path must lead to light”, I assured myself;
“It is followed by everyone”.
I sensed a thousand openings,
But ventured into none.

Everyday a little closer,
To the illusionary light.
Everyday a little farther from,
The escape hidden in sight.

And every now and then
An inquisitive mole,
Tired and hopeless,
Jumped out of a hole.

And there were those
Whose faith had seen an end.
Who accepted the darkness,
And stopped to pretend.

You could find a soul like this
On every other turn;
Who united with the light of death,
When its body was left to burn.

I reminded myself everyday,
Of the light I would see.
Little by little, I embraced the dark;
My only company.

But one day I felt a blinding light,
One I could almost see.
I peered out of the hole
And though if I should flee.

Everyone had dismissed it
As any other hole.
Why then it had thrust,
Its impression on my soul?

Keeping aside my doubts,
I followed my heart’s call.
Light or no-light,
It would end once and for all.

Ahead of me the merriest world,
And on my side there lay;
A cloth loosened by the fall,
The blindfold I had all the way.
