Tulip Pandey

Moonlit Night

The Earth slowly and silently,
Enters into the realms of dream.
Stepping into the black bleakness,
Some find spooky, some serene.

Tossing and turning in the bed of stars
It finally cuddles into a side.
Pleasantly aware of days gone by,
Of times it has laughed and times it has cried.

With a face beaming, golden-bright;
The Earth so fresh,so pure and chaste.
Lets down it's black silky hair
To the luminous face, much in contrast

The cherished gem, in it's ear, nonetheless
Sends silver streaks down the hair,
Inspired by the brightest, fiery face
Forming pearls and beads that twinkle and glare.

Encompassing the beauty the night beholds,
Sending an enlightening spark;
The grey-black locks of Venus unfold,
Revealing the gem that lights the dark.
