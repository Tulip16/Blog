Sole Soul

They stared in awe at the sparkling sea,
Which ,in varying shades, the light did paint.
They heard different types of sounds;
The chirping -clear;the sobbing- faint.

They were all jealous of the girl;
The sole soul in the splendid sea.
In the waves who got to roll and swirl,
They didn't know what for her it'd be.

They knew; the one who had the right,
To bask in the glorious sea;
Was the one who could muster the might
To fight the waves with bravery.

They thought that she was bold and brave ,
Worthy of all the pleasures she had.
Little did they really know ,
She was anything but glad.
They wondered if there was a ship,
That in this ocean did not sail.
Only one called friendship did she know,
In every attempt,it only did fail.

They say small things are magical,
And she couldn't agree more.
This sea was indeed made of drops;
That flowed from her eyes when times were sore.

For she was the creator of the sea,
She wouldn't drown even in a faulty boat.
For it reminded her of sorrows and pains,
She didn't wish to stay afloat.

She wished to swim with deadly pace,
And reach the shore where she could see.
Perhaps a smiling friendly face,
Who knew what she was and could be.
